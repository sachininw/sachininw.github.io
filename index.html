Sachini Weerasekara

weerasekara.s [AT] northeastern [DOT] edu

I am a PhD student in Machine Learning at Northeastern University, where I am fortunate to be co-advised by Prof. Jacqueline Isaacs and Prof. Sagar Kamarthi in the Department of Mechanical & Industrial Engineering. I previously earned my master’s degree at Northeastern University and my bachelor’s degree from the University of Moratuwa in Sri Lanka.

I have also spent time doing machine learning research at Dell Technologies, Takeda Pharmaceuticals and Aikium Inc . Prior to graduate school, I worked full-time at EFL Global for few years, developing robotic process automation tools with machine learning.

My current research focuses on learning representations of complex dynamical systems to enable autonomous control and downstream applications. I approach these challenges through the lens of use-driven machine learning. To this end, my work centers on two main thrusts: (1) developing machine learning methods to learn robust, structure-aware representations that capture multi-scale dynamics of complex systems, including those arising in human biology and engineering systems; and (2) leveraging these representations to enable prediction, control, and explainability under real-world constraints.
